2. Time Management Strategy

With 180 minutes for 65 questions, you have approximately 2.77 minutes per question on average. However, not all questions take equal time.

2.1 Recommended Time Allocation

Question TypeMax Time (1-mark)Max Time (2-mark)

Rule of Thumb: If you have spent more than 4 minutes on a 1-mark question, mark it for review and move on. Similarly, cap 2-mark questions at 6-7 minutes maximum.

3. Question Attempt Order Strategy

The order in which you attempt questions can significantly impact your score.

3.1 Recommended Approach

  1. Start with General Aptitude: These are usually straightforward and build confidence. Target 12-15 marks in 15-20 minutes.
  2. Move to your strongest subject: Tackle the subject you are most confident in next. This secures easy marks early.
  3. Sweep all 1-mark questions: Go through all 1-mark technical questions. Attempt easy ones, mark uncertain ones.
  4. Tackle 2-mark questions by strength: Go subject by subject in order of your confidence level.
  5. Return to marked questions: Use remaining time for flagged questions.
  6. Final sweep for NATs: Ensure every NAT has an answer entered.

4. Handling Difficult Questions

4.1 The 3-Step Rule

  1. Read once carefully. Understand exactly what is being asked. Many "difficult" questions are just poorly read.
  2. Spend 60 seconds thinking. If you see a path to the solution, continue. If not, mark and move.
  3. On return, use elimination. For MCQs, try to eliminate 2 options. Then guess from remaining if needed.

4.2 When to Guess on MCQs

Options EliminatedExpected Value (1-mark)Action
0 (random guess)+1/4 - 1/3 = -0.08Do NOT guess
1 eliminated+1/3 - 1/3 = 0Borderline; guess if gut feeling
2 eliminated+1/2 - 1/3 = +0.17GUESS (positive expected value)
3 eliminated+1Obviously answer
For 2-mark MCQs: The math is similar. Guess when you can eliminate 2 or more options. Expected value with 2 eliminated: +1 - 2/3 = +0.33.

4.3 Dealing with Anxiety During the Exam

  • If you encounter a series of hard questions, do not panic. The exam has easy questions scattered throughout.
  • Take a 30-second deep breath break if you feel overwhelmed.
  • Remember: you do not need to solve all 65 questions. Solving 45-50 correctly with minimal negative marking can yield a top score.

6. Last Month Revision Plan

The final month before GATE should be dedicated to consolidation, not new learning.

6.1 Week-by-Week Plan

WeekActivityDetails
Week 1 (Day 1-7)Subject Revision Round 1Revise all subjects using notes. 2 subjects per day. Focus on formulas and key concepts.
Week 2 (Day 8-14)Previous Year PapersSolve last 5 years' papers in exam conditions (3-hour timed sessions). Analyze mistakes.
Week 3 (Day 15-21)Weak Area Focus + MocksTake 3-4 full-length mock tests. Spend remaining time on your 2-3 weakest topics.
Week 4 (Day 22-28)Formula Revision + Light PracticeRevise formula sheets daily. Solve 20-30 easy-medium problems daily. No new topics.
Day 29-30Rest & Light ReviewLight formula review only. Rest well. Prepare exam day logistics.

6.3 Mock Test Strategy

  • Take at least 8-10 full-length mocks in the last month.
  • After each mock, spend 1-2 hours analyzing mistakes.
  • Categorize mistakes: silly errors, concept gaps, time management issues.
  • Maintain an error log and revise it weekly.
  • Track your scores to see improvement trend.

7. Exam Day Checklist

  • Carry admit card and valid photo ID.
  • Reach the center at least 60 minutes early.
  • Eat a light meal; avoid heavy food that causes drowsiness.
  • Use the on-screen calculator provided; no physical calculators allowed.
  • Use the virtual scientific calculator for complex calculations.
  • Stay hydrated but do not overdrink water.
  • Do not discuss difficult questions during breaks (if any); stay focused on your own performance.
Final Reminder: The difference between a good score and a great score is often not knowledge but strategy. Practice these strategies during every mock test so they become second nature on exam day.

General Aptitude for GATE

📝 General Aptitude

General Aptitude carries 15 marks in GATE (5 questions of 1-mark + 5 questions of 2-marks). This section is common across all GATE papers and is often the easiest to score in with proper preparation.

1. Verbal Ability

1.1 Grammar Rules

  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Singular subject takes singular verb. "The group of students is (not are) studying."
  • Tense Consistency: Do not switch tenses within a sentence unless the time frame changes.
  • Articles: "a" before consonant sounds, "an" before vowel sounds. "A university" (not "an") because "u" sounds like "yoo".
  • Commonly confused words: affect/effect, principal/principle, complement/compliment, stationary/stationery.

Example Problem: Choose the correct sentence:

(A) Neither of the students have completed the assignment.

(B) Neither of the students has completed the assignment.

Answer: (B) — "Neither" is singular and takes a singular verb "has."

1.2 Sentence Completion

Look for contextual clues in the sentence. Identify if the blank needs a positive, negative, or neutral word.

Example: The politician's speech was so ______ that even his supporters were bored.

(A) eloquent (B) monotonous (C) inspiring (D) brief

Answer: (B) monotonous — The clue "even his supporters were bored" indicates the speech was dull.

1.3 Verbal Analogies

Identify the relationship between the first pair, then apply the same relationship to find the answer.

Example: Pen : Writer :: Scalpel : ?

(A) Carpenter (B) Surgeon (C) Soldier (D) Painter

Answer: (B) Surgeon — A pen is the tool of a writer; a scalpel is the tool of a surgeon.

1.4 Reading Comprehension

  • Read the questions first, then read the passage with those questions in mind.
  • Look for the main idea (usually in the first and last paragraphs).
  • For "inference" questions, choose what is logically implied, not explicitly stated.
  • Beware of answer choices that are true in general but not supported by the passage.

2. Numerical Ability

2.1 Percentages

Key Formulas:

  • Percentage change = [(New - Old) / Old] * 100
  • If a value increases by x%, new value = Original * (1 + x/100)
  • If a value decreases by x%, new value = Original * (1 - x/100)
  • Successive % changes: Overall change = a + b + ab/100 (for two changes a% and b%)

Example: A product's price increases by 20% and then decreases by 10%. What is the net change?

Solution: Net change = 20 + (-10) + (20)(-10)/100 = 20 - 10 - 2 = 8% increase

2.2 Ratios and Proportions

  • If a:b = 2:3 and b:c = 4:5, then a:b:c = 8:12:15 (equalize the common term b)
  • Directly proportional: y = kx. Inversely proportional: y = k/x

Example: The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:5. If there are 40 students, how many boys?

Solution: Boys = (3/8) * 40 = 15 boys

2.3 Time, Speed, and Distance

Example: A car travels 120 km at 60 km/h and returns at 40 km/h. Find average speed.

Solution: Average speed = 2*60*40 / (60+40) = 4800/100 = 48 km/h

2.4 Work Problems

  • If A can do work in 'a' days, A's rate = 1/a per day
  • Combined rate: 1/a + 1/b = (a+b)/(ab). Together they finish in ab/(a+b) days.

Example: A can complete a task in 12 days, B in 18 days. Working together?

Solution: Combined rate = 1/12 + 1/18 = 5/36. Days = 36/5 = 7.2 days

2.6 Algebra

  • Quadratic formula: x = [-b +/- sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)] / 2a
  • Sum of AP: S_n = n/2 * [2a + (n-1)d]
  • Sum of GP: S_n = a(r^n - 1)/(r - 1) for r ≠ 1
  • Sum of first n natural numbers: n(n+1)/2
  • Sum of squares: n(n+1)(2n+1)/6

3. Data Interpretation

Questions present data in tables, bar charts, pie charts, or line graphs, and ask you to calculate values from the data.

3.1 Tips for Data Interpretation

  • Read the title, axis labels, and legends carefully before solving.
  • For pie charts: each percentage of total = (angle/360) * 100
  • Approximate when possible. Exact calculation is often unnecessary.
  • Watch for units: "in thousands" or "in lakhs" can change answers by orders of magnitude.

Example: A pie chart shows: Marketing 30%, R&D 25%, Operations 35%, Admin 10%. Total budget = Rs. 20 lakhs. Find R&D budget.

Solution: R&D = 25% of 20 = Rs. 5 lakhs

4. Logical Reasoning

4.1 Puzzles and Seating Arrangement

Draw diagrams. For circular seating, fix one person's position and place others relative to them.

Example: Six people A, B, C, D, E, F sit in a row. B sits next to A. C is not at either end. D sits at one end. Who can sit at the other end?

Approach: Fix D at one end. Place C not at the other end. B must be next to A. Systematically try arrangements.

4.2 Blood Relations

Tip: Draw a family tree. Use M/F labels for gender. Connect relationships with arrows.

4.3 Direction Sense

  • Draw a compass (N, S, E, W) and trace the path step by step.
  • "Turning right from North" = East. "Turning left from East" = North.

4.4 Coding-Decoding

Example: If COMPUTER = DPNQVUFS, what is GATE?

Solution: Each letter is shifted by +1 (C+1=D, O+1=P, ...). GATE = HBUF

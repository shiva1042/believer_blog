4. Pumping Lemma

For Regular Languages

  1. Assume L is regular. Then there exists pumping length p.
  2. Choose a string w in L with |w| ≥ p.
  3. For ALL decompositions w = xyz with |xy| ≤ p and |y| ≥ 1:
  4. Show xy^i z is NOT in L for some i ≥ 0.
  5. Contradiction → L is not regular.

For Context-Free Languages

  1. Assume L is CFL. Then there exists pumping length p.
  2. Choose w in L with |w| ≥ p.
  3. For ALL decompositions w = uvxyz with |vxy| ≤ p, |vy| ≥ 1:
  4. Show uv^i xy^i z is NOT in L for some i ≥ 0.
  5. Contradiction → L is not CFL.
Critical: Pumping lemma can only DISPROVE membership (show a language is NOT regular/CFL). It CANNOT prove a language IS regular/CFL.

6. Key Undecidable Problems

  • Halting Problem: Does TM M halt on input w? (Undecidable, but RE)
  • Rice's Theorem: Every non-trivial property of the language of a TM is undecidable.
  • Post Correspondence Problem (PCP): Undecidable.
  • Equivalence of TMs: Undecidable.
  • L(M) = {}? for TM M: Undecidable.

7. Quick Facts

  • NFA to DFA: max 2^n states. Minimum DFA is unique (up to isomorphism).
  • Regular: closed under ALL boolean and regular operations.
  • CFL: NOT closed under intersection and complement.
  • DCFL: closed under complement but NOT union, intersection, or concatenation.
  • Every DCFL has an unambiguous grammar but not vice versa.
  • L is recursive iff both L and L-complement are RE.
  • If L is RE and L-complement is RE, then L is recursive (decidable).
Most Tested: Closure properties table, pumping lemma application, decidability of emptiness/membership for regular/CFL, and Rice's theorem.
